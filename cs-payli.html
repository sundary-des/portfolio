Payli — Payments & Bills Tracker

Olga Kalinina · Lead Product & UX/UI Designer · Feb 2025

A bill tracking app that helps you keep bills & payments in one place. Add bills once and the app reminds you — so nothing slips through the cracks — and helps you manage recurring payment options for control.

Project snapshot

Studio: Freelance Project, 2025
Timeline: 4 weeks
My role: Lead Product & UX/UI Designer
Key brief: "Payli"
Platform: iOS + Android


An app for tracking payments, bills, and subscriptions in one place. Designed to help busy people manage recurring payments, track spending by category and project, and stay on top of due dates — with minimal friction and smart notification options for each bill.

Tools

Figma Notion Gemini Claude Amplitude

The Problem

People who manage bills, subscriptions and recurring payments for themselves and their families have no intuitive tool that solves the task simply, completely and quickly.

The key pain:

  • Bills are scattered across apps, emails, and paper statements
  • Easy to forget due dates → late fees, service interruptions
  • No simple way to track who owes what across multiple currencies
  • Existing tools are complex: they're built for accountants, not everyday life

Product goal

Help busy people keep bills & payments on track by:

  • Adding and categorising payment entries in under 30 seconds
  • Smartly grouping recurring bills by status (due, overdue, paid, scheduled)
  • Supporting multiple projects and currencies in a single view
  • Sending timely, customisable reminders to never miss a bill again

User & Research

Preliminary goals & key metrics:


Business metrics:

  • Increase daily active users (DAU)
  • Reduce churn by improving habit-forming actions
  • Grow premium subscription conversion

UX metrics:

  • Task success rate for adding a bill: target > 90%
  • Time-on-task: add bill in under 30 seconds
  • Improve notification opt-in rate
  • Reduce drop-off at the billing summary screen

Keep bills organised & Payments on track

The design focused on making bill entry fast and intuitive, keeping the home screen clean, and ensuring that due dates, statuses, and amounts were scannable at a glance. Each entry is categorised, annotated, and optionally tagged to a project or recurring schedule — giving users full visibility without cognitive overload.

Payments and Transactions Screen

The primary screen shows all entries grouped by date. Each entry shows: category, title, transaction type (income/expense), amount across multiple currencies, status tag (Paid, Due, Overdue, Scheduled, Cancelled), and optional metadata like project, recurring frequency, and notes.

The colour-coded status tags allow users to scan and prioritise immediately — without opening each entry. The left accent bar adds a visual hierarchy layer to distinguish entries at a glance.

Note Preview Screen

Tapping an entry opens a full-screen note preview with a structured detail view: type, status, amounts per currency, project assignment, recurring schedule, attachments count, and free-text note field.

The design keeps this screen lightweight — no heavy modals, no context switching. Edit and Close CTAs are persistent at the bottom, keeping user control clear at all times.

Built for busy people managing bills across Projects and Currencies

Throughout the project, the focus was on reducing cognitive load for people who manage payments across multiple accounts, currencies, and contexts — whether it's household bills, freelance client projects, or shared expenses. The result is a structured yet lightweight app that keeps payments visible, organised, and under control.
